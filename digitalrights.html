01

The Problem

Remember that movie you "bought" on Vudu? That album in your Google Play library? That show you watched a dozen times? One day — no warning — it's just gone. Pulled. Delisted. Because you never actually owned it. You rented the right to watch it.

Streaming platforms remove content constantly. Studios fight over licensing. Services shut down. Your digital "purchases" are licenses — and licenses can be revoked at any time for any reason. Your photos on iCloud, Google Photos? Those live on someone else's hardware, subject to someone else's terms of service that change without warning.

Even ebooks from Amazon can vanish from your Kindle overnight. No refund. No appeal. Just gone. DRM — Digital Rights Management — is the lock corporations put on the media you think you own. It's a padlock on your own bookshelf that only they hold the key to.

The Solution — Run Your Own Stack

Self-hosting sounds technical. It isn't — not anymore. These are open-source tools built by people who believe your data belongs to you. Once they're running on your hardware, no company can pull the plug. No subscription. No one watching what you watch.

Jellyfin

Stream your entire movie and TV library to any device — phone, TV, tablet, laptop — from anywhere. Free. Open source. No subscription. Nobody ever pulls your library.

Immich
// Your Google Photos

Navidrome
// Your Spotify

Calibre
// Your Bookshelf

03

The Hardware — That Old Laptop Is a Server

Got an old laptop from 2015? A desktop you were about to recycle? A spare tower collecting dust in the garage? That's a server. Most home media setups run beautifully on machines people throw away.

We're talking an old i5 processor, 8GB of RAM, a couple of hard drives. That's it. You don't need new hardware. You don't need a rack. You need the right setup on the machine you've already got.

// MINIMUM_VIABLE_SERVER CPU ............. Old Intel i5 or AMD equivalent
RAM ............. 8 GB (more = better for Immich)
Storage ......... 1–4 TB HDD (old drives work fine)
OS .............. Ubuntu Server or TrueNAS
Cost ............ ~$0 if you already have the hardware

Before you take that machine to the recycler — stop. Consider what it can become. A personal cloud. A media server. A photo vault. A smart home hub. The hardware you were going to throw away is someone else's enterprise solution.
